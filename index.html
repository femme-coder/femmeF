Types of emotional abuse in teenagers and children

  1. Rejecting- this is putting down a youngster's worth or putting down their needs.
  2. Ignoring- this is failing to give any response or interraction with a child or youth at all.
  3. Isolation- keeping away from family and peers.
  4. Terrorising- causing a child or youth to be terrified by the constant use of threats and or intimidating behaviour.
  5. Corrupting- encouraging a child or youth to do things that are illegal or harmful to themselves.
  6. Exploiting- giving a child or youth responsibilities that are far greater than a chlid/youth that age can handle. it is also using a child for profit.
  7. Verbal abuse- this is the most common type of emotional abuse which includes:
  • Name calling
  • Condescension
  • Criticism and others

Some individuals experience emotional abuse at home, at schools,in the society and in relationships.It is worth mentioning that some caretakers in family households may inadvertedly abuse the chidren as a way of diffusing whatever difficulties they had during their upbringing. In schools, "bullies" are the most common oppressors but teachers also play a role in the abuse as they sometimes belittle them.Most communities tend to mock peope with diffrent lifestyles and/or their physical appearances.When some partners in emotionally abusive relationships have disagreements, the abusive partner uses offensive language and make the other partner feel bad about themselves by using gaslighting.


Possible signs of emotional abuse in youngsters

  • youngsters withdrawn from and uninterested in ordinary activities
  • unexpected and unexplained mood swings
  • demonstrated fear of upsetting their partner
  • reluctance to engage in activities without their partner,for fear of retribution.
  • low self-esteem and self-worth
  • drug and alcohol abuse
  • self-harm and/or suicidal behaviours

Effects of emotional abuse

Suicide
Some victims resort to suicide when they realise that they cannot take the abuse anymore or when not believed when reporting the abuse. This usually happens at a point where depression has taken over.

Isolation
An abused person tends to withdraw themselves from their peers and everyone else because the abuse has totally shattered their social intelligence thus getting social anxiety.

Drug Abuse
The victims may end up abusing drugs to escape the sad reality of not being able to defend themselves from their perpetrators

Long-term effects
Not only does the abuse have short-term effects,it also has long-term effects like the abused being paranoid,feeling guilty about everything that goes wrong, deteorating one's self-esteem.
